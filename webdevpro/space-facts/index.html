Our Solar System Planets

Journey through the eight worlds of our cosmic neighborhood

Mercury

Closest to Sun 58M km

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. A year on Mercury lasts only 88 Earth days, but a day on Mercury lasts 59 Earth days.

Earth

Our Home 150M km

Earth is the only known planet with life. It has liquid water on its surface and a protective atmosphere. Earth is about 4.5 billion years old and has one natural satellite - the Moon.

Only planet with life

Mars

Red Planet 228M km

Mars is known as the "Red Planet" due to iron oxide on its surface. It has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, and evidence suggests it once had liquid water.

Largest volcano: Olympus Mons

Jupiter

Gas Giant 778M km

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system. It has a Great Red Spot, which is a giant storm larger than Earth. Jupiter has over 80 moons, including the four largest discovered by Galileo.

Great Red Spot storm

Saturn

Ringed Planet 1.4B km

Saturn is famous for its beautiful ring system made of ice and rock particles. It's less dense than water, so it would float if there were an ocean big enough to hold it.

Less dense than water

Uranus

Ice Giant 2.9B km

Uranus rotates on its side, making it unique among planets. It's an ice giant with a tilted axis of 98 degrees and has faint rings that were discovered in 1977.

Rotates on its side

Neptune

Windiest Planet 4.5B km

Neptune is the windiest planet with speeds reaching up to 1,200 mph (2,000 km/h). It's the farthest planet from the Sun and takes 165 Earth years to complete one orbit.

Winds up to 2,000 km/h

Stars & Galaxies

Explore the magnificent cosmic structures beyond our solar system

Stars

Nuclear Fusion

Stars are massive, glowing balls of gas that produce light and heat through nuclear fusion. Our Sun is a medium-sized star, and there are billions of stars in our galaxy alone. Stars are born in nebulae and can live for millions to billions of years.

Brightness Level

The Milky Way

100,000 light-years

The Milky Way is our home galaxy, containing over 100 billion stars. It's a spiral galaxy about 100,000 light-years across. Our solar system is located in one of the spiral arms, about 26,000 light-years from the galactic center.

Exploration Progress

Black Holes

Infinite Gravity

Black holes are regions in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. They form when massive stars collapse at the end of their lives. The closest black hole to Earth is about 1,000 light-years away.

Mystery Level

Space Exploration

Humanity's journey beyond Earth and into the cosmos

Satellites

Since 1957

Thousands of satellites orbit Earth, helping us with communication, weather forecasting, GPS navigation, and scientific research. The first artificial satellite, Sputnik 1, was launched by the Soviet Union in 1957.

First: Sputnik 1

Rockets

Evolving

Rockets are vehicles that carry spacecraft and astronauts into space. They work by burning fuel and pushing hot gases out of their engines. Modern rockets can be reused, making space travel more affordable.

Reusable Technology

Astronauts

Since 1961

Astronauts are specially trained people who travel to space. The first human in space was Yuri Gagarin in 1961, and the first person to walk on the Moon was Neil Armstrong in 1969. Today, astronauts live and work on the International Space Station.

Moon Landing 1969

Mind-Blowing Space Facts

Prepare to be amazed by these incredible cosmic truths

01

Venus Backwards Day

A day on Venus (243 Earth days) is actually longer than a year on Venus (225 Earth days) because it rotates so slowly on its axis.

243 days = 1 Venus day
02

Space Silence

Space is completely silent because there's no air or atmosphere to carry sound waves. Astronauts must use radios to communicate with each other.

No sound in vacuum
03

Floating Water

In the zero gravity of space, water forms perfect spheres and floats around. Astronauts have to be careful when drinking because the water can float away and damage equipment.

Perfect water spheres
04

Diamond Rain

Scientists believe it rains diamonds on Jupiter and Saturn. The extreme pressure and carbon in their atmospheres can create diamond particles that fall like rain.

Literal diamond precipitation
05

Eternal Footprints

The footprints left by Apollo astronauts on the Moon will last for millions of years because there's no wind or water to erode them away.

Millions of years preserved
